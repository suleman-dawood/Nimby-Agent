## What's being proposed

This planning proposal, known as the Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.12], aims to deliver a new community facility and library within the Seven Hills Town Centre [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.5][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.14]. This facility is designed to accommodate residential, commercial, and community uses, providing essential social services and shared spaces [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.23][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.14]. The facility will also feature publicly accessible areas, including a pedestrian plaza and new open space [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.5][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.23][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.17]. To enable this, the proposal involves changing the land zoning and classification [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.38][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.12]. Some areas are proposed to be rezoned to E1 Local Centre, allowing for a mix of residential, commercial, and community uses [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.23]. Another key part is a land swap with Homes NSW [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.23]. This change will allow Homes NSW to develop future social and affordable housing in the area [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.23][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.15].

## What changes on the ground

The proposal significantly changes planning controls for several land parcels. A key change is the rezoning of 2,322 square metres of land from RE1 Public Recreation to E1 Local Centre, effectively removing the RE1 zone from the site [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.27]. Specifically, Lot 201 (918 sqm) and part of Lot 194 (229 sqm) will change from RE1 Public Recreation to E1 Local Centre [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.27]. Lots 141 and 142 (1,175 sqm combined) will shift from R4 High Density Residential to E1 Local Centre [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.27]. Another part of Lot 194 (1,175 sqm) will change from RE1 Public Recreation to R4 High Density Residential, ensuring the total R4 land area remains constant [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.27]. Height limits will also be adjusted. Parcel A increases from 14 to 16 metres [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.20][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.11]. Parcel B, currently with no limit, will be capped at 16 metres [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.20][doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.11]. Parcel C will have new limits of 16 metres (C1) and 20 metres (C2) [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.20]. For the 2,322 sqm previously RE1, the new E1 zoning and 16-metre height limit would reduce maximum potential developable area from an assumed 11,610 sqm to 9,288 sqm [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.32][doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.29][doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.30]. These changes aim to facilitate a flexible, multipurpose community hub [doc: Draft Seven Hills Community Hub Planning Proposal | p.23].

## Things to know

Here are some things to know about the planning proposal: * **Flooding:** The site is located within the Blacktown Creek catchment, approximately 180 metres north of the creek [doc: 250711 Seven Hills CRH - Flood Impact and Risk Assessment - 241313 | p.11]. A Flood Impact and Risk Assessment indicates the site is potentially at risk of mainstream flooding from the creek, as well as overland and flash flooding from local stormwater [doc: 250711 Seven Hills CRH - Flood Impact and Risk Assessment - 241313 | p.11]. A flood advice letter is also required [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.40]. * **Heritage:** A heritage impact assessment concludes the proposed two-storey building will not affect the significance of nearby heritage items [doc: 240443 - Heritage Impact Assessment | p.20]. The assessment did not cover Aboriginal or archaeological heritage [doc: 240443 - Heritage Impact Assessment | p.8]. * **Contamination & Remediation:** Any remediation work will involve managing health and safety risks, including controls for noise, dust, and odours [doc: Seven Hills Community Hub-Draft RAP | p.35]. * **Noise & Traffic:** A traffic study is required to assess potential traffic impacts [doc: R_Traffic_4_Boomerang_Pl_Planning Proposal | p.40]. * **Trees:** (No information supported by the provided excerpts)
