## What's being proposed

The planning proposal for 365 Marshall Mount Road, Marshall Mount seeks to redevelop the site into a new integrated residential neighbourhood [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.7]. This is in response to the broader NSW housing crisis and aims to provide more housing opportunities [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.8].

The main change being proposed is to amend the Wollongong Local Environmental Plan 2009 [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.7]. Specifically, it seeks to reduce the minimum lot size for the C4 Environmental Living zoned land on the site [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2]. The current minimum lot size is 4,999m², and the proposal aims to change this to 999m² [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.7][doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2].

This change is primarily driven by the availability of a sewer connection to the site, which was not expected when the larger lot size was originally established [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.7][doc: Frequently Asked Questions | p.3]. The proposal includes an "Indicative Concept Scheme" that demonstrates how this change could facilitate the creation of 72 residential lots [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2].

## What changes on the ground

The main change proposed for the 12-hectare part of the site at 365 Marshall Mount Road, which is zoned C4 Environmental Living, is a reduction in the minimum lot size [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1][doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2]. Currently, lots in this area must be at least 4,999m² [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.27][doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2]. The proposal seeks to reduce this minimum to 999m² [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2]. This change would allow for about 72 residential lots, according to an indicative concept scheme [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2]. The new lots would vary in size, ranging from 1,000m² to 1,800m², 2,000m² to 3,300m², and 5,000m² to 6,000m² [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.31]. There are no proposed changes to the existing C4 Environmental Living zoning for this portion of the site [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1][doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.36]. The maximum building height of 9m [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.26] and floor space ratio (which has no maximum for this zone) [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.27] would remain unchanged. The land is expected to be serviced by Sydney Water [doc: Planning Proposal Report - 23 March 2026 | p.35]. The increase in lots is estimated to generate an additional 377 vehicle movements per day [doc: PP Appendix F - Traffic Assessment | p.21].

## Things to know

Here are some key findings from the technical studies for the proposal at 365 Marshall Mount Road:

The site is identified as 'bushfire prone' [doc: PP Appendix D - Bushfire Report | p.11] and is mapped as Category 3 Vegetation [doc: PP Appendix D - Bushfire Report | p.11]. All future development must comply with the requirements of 'Planning for Bush Fire Protection - 2019' [doc: PP Appendix D - Bushfire Report | p.11].

Regarding heritage, there are no listed heritage items within the study area or 1km of it [doc: PP Appendix I - Non-Aboriginal Heritage Report | p.34]. The proposal will not physically or visually impact any listed heritage items [doc: PP Appendix I - Non-Aboriginal Heritage Report | p.34], and no specific non-Aboriginal heritage values were identified for the area [doc: PP Appendix I - Non-Aboriginal Heritage Report | p.35].

However, proposed ground-disturbing works, including bulk earthworks and construction of roads and utilities, will directly impact one Aboriginal archaeological site (AHIMS 52-5-1051/Marshall Mount Road AF), resulting in its total loss of value [doc: PP Appendix K - Archaeological Report | p.64]. Another Aboriginal site and four areas of archaeological potential will be avoided [doc: PP Appendix K - Archaeological Report | p.64]. An Unexpected Finds Policy for both non-Aboriginal and Aboriginal heritage must be in place before any ground disturbance begins [doc: PP Appendix I - Non-Aboriginal Heritage Report | p.4]. Aboriginal archaeological remains are protected by the National Parks and Wildlife Act 1974 [doc: PP Appendix I - Non-Aboriginal Heritage Report | p.4].
