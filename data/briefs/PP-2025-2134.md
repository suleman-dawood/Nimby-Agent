# Reclassification and Rezoning of Surplus Council Sites

**PP Number:** PP-2025-2134  
**Council:** Port Stephens  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-13 to 2026-05-11  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

Port Stephens Council is proposing changes to eight Council-owned sites across Raymond Terrace, Anna Bay, Fingal Bay, and Medowie [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.1][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.4].

The main aim of this proposal is to allow these sites to be sold by the Council and used for residential purposes [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.18]. This will create opportunities for additional housing in existing areas [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.38].

Two key changes are being proposed:
Firstly, all eight sites are proposed to be reclassified from "Community" land to "Operational" land [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.18][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.5]. This reclassification would enable the Council to dispose of the sites [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.5][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.18].

Secondly, four of these eight sites are also proposed to be rezoned [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.18][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.5]. These four sites (1B Jessica Close, 4 Payton Street, 20 Benjamin Lee Drive in Raymond Terrace, and 27 Campbell Avenue in Anna Bay) would change from their current "RE1 Public Recreation" zoning to "R2 Low Density Residential" [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.5][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.4]. The remaining four sites already have an "R2 Low Density Residential" zoning, which they will retain [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.5][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.4].

These sites were identified as surplus by the Council after a strategic review, including the Recreation Strategy adopted in 2018 [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.5][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.38].

## What changes on the ground

This proposal involves changes to how several Council-owned sites can be used. All eight sites are proposed to be reclassified from "Community" land to "Operational" land [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.4]. This reclassification means the sites would no longer be reserved for public purposes and could be sold or used for other Council activities [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.32]. For 124 Benjamin Lee Drive, Raymond Terrace, specifically the southern rectangular part of the property would be reclassified to Operational land [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.39].

Currently, four sites – 1B Jessica Close, 4 Payton Street, and 20 Benjamin Lee Drive in Raymond Terrace, and 27 Campbell Avenue, Anna Bay – are zoned RE1 Public Recreation [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.21][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.22][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.23][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.24]. The proposal seeks to change their zoning to R2 Low Density Residential [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.21][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.22][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.23][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.24]. For these four sites, new controls would apply: a maximum building height of 9 metres [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.21][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.22][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.23][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.24] and a minimum lot size of 500 square metres [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.21][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.22][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.23][doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.24]. The remaining four sites already have R2 Low Density Residential zoning and will not have these specific map changes [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.39].

For 154 Rocky Point Road, Fingal Bay, which currently lacks direct road access, the proposal suggests a driveway could be provided through an adjoining Council Road Reserve if the site is sold [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.32]. Environmental considerations, such as keeping koala feed trees and avoiding flood paths, are conditions for the proposal to move forward [doc: Stage 1.07 Planning Proposal_v6 - Exhibition version | p.39].

## Things to know

Here are some key things to know about the planning proposal: The proposal includes environmental considerations, particularly for local vegetation and wildlife. At 4 Payton Street, Raymond Terrace, plans involve removing up to three Forest Red Gum trees and up to three hollow-bearing trees [doc: Ecological Assessment Report (Wildthing, December 2025) | p.83]. Additionally, approximately three Blackbutt trees are expected to be removed from 154 Rocky Point Road, Fingal Bay [doc: Ecological Assessment Report (Wildthing, December 2025) | p.83]. The project will also remove habitat for a number of threatened species [doc: Ecological Assessment Report (Wildthing, December 2025) | p.83]. Potential indirect impacts during development include erosion and sedimentation, possible restrictions on native animal movement, and an increased spread of weeds [doc: Ecological Assessment Report (Wildthing, December 2025) | p.83]. Although some sites are located within or near internationally significant wetlands, the proposal is not expected to impact these areas [doc: Ecological Assessment Report (Wildthing, December 2025) | p.109]. Plans are in place to minimise impacts on native vegetation and protect biodiversity [doc: Ecological Assessment Report (Wildthing, December 2025) | p.83]. For Aboriginal heritage, all Aboriginal objects are protected by law, even if they are not recorded in the AHIMS database, which may not always be current or complete [doc: AHIMS Searches_Combined | p.2].
