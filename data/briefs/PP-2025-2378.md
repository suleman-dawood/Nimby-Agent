# Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (CURA A)

**PP Number:** PP-2025-2378  
**Council:** Not specified  
**Address:** ["658 Tamarind Drive Cumbalum 2478"]  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-07 to 2026-05-08  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

This proposal aims to extend the Cumbalum Urban Release Area A at 658 Tamarind Drive, Cumbalum [Context]. It involves changing the zoning of the inner site from rural (RU1 Primary Production and RU2 Rural Landscape) to a mix of urban and environmental zones [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.8][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.8]. These new zones include R2 Low Density Residential, R3 Medium Density Residential, RE1 Public Recreation, and C3 Environmental Management, which will allow for increased housing diversity and choice [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.8][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.8][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.45].

The proposal also introduces new minimum lot sizes for both the inner site and a 20-hectare minimum for the outer site [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.8][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.8]. Supporting this, Ballina LEP 2012 maps, including the Land Zoning Map and Lot Size Map, will be updated [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.28][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.26].

Future subdivisions will include low-voltage electricity and telecommunications, extending services from Ballina Heights Drive [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.28][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.26]. Additionally, amendments to Ballina Development Control Plan 2012 Chapter 3 Urban Subdivision are proposed to guide urban subdivision [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.8][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.8].

## What changes on the ground

The planning proposal introduces new specific controls for the area.

Portions of the site will be zoned R2 Low Density Residential, with a minimum lot size of 1200m2 [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.14][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.45]. Other parts will be zoned R3 Medium Density Residential, with varying minimum lot sizes of 450m2 for less sloped areas, and 800m2 for areas with noise and slope constraints [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.14][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2 Gateway) | p.12][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.45]. For the residue sections of Lot 363, which is the outer part of the site, a new minimum lot size of 20 hectares will apply [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.14][doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.6].

The proposal also includes plans for an internal road layout [doc: Appendix 5 -Traffic Impact Assessment | p.12] and requires the incorporation of a 4-meter high acoustic barrier in some areas [doc: BSCPP 2024-006 Planning Proposal - Extension of Cumbalum Urban Release Area A (V2.2 Exhibition) | p.14]. Building heights and floor space ratios are intended to integrate with the area's planned urban form [doc: Appendix O - Coastal Design Guidelines | p.4].

## Things to know

Residents should note key findings from technical studies. A bushfire study confirms the development is on the urban edge of Ballina Heights with safe evacuation routes available [doc: Appendix N - Bushfire Study | p.22]. New water, electricity, and gas services must meet specific bushfire protection standards, with a hydraulic consultant to confirm the hydrant system design [doc: Appendix N - Bushfire Study | p.22]. While fire runs from the west are the most likely impact, they are limited to 60-100 meters and disconnected by roads, which will reduce fire intensity [doc: Appendix N - Bushfire Study | p.16]. New power lines should be underground [doc: Appendix N - Bushfire Study | p.23], and the development is expected to reduce fuel loads for adjacent properties [doc: Appendix N - Bushfire Study | p.23].

A road traffic noise assessment considered impacts from the Pacific Motorway and Ballina Heights Drive [doc: Appendix E - Acoustic Report | p.14]. Properties identified as noise-affected will have restrictions placed on their titles [doc: Appendix E - Acoustic Report | p.11][doc: Appendix 8 - Draft DCP Amendments | p.11]. This ensures future buildings meet internal noise goals [doc: Appendix E - Acoustic Report | p.11]. Acoustic barriers up to 4 metres high may be needed near the Pacific Motorway in noise-impacted areas [doc: Appendix 8 - Draft DCP Amendments | p.11].
