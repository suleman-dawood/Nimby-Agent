# Planning Proposal - Derriwong Road and Old Northern Road, Dural

**PP Number:** PP-2024-450  
**Council:** Not specified  
**Address:** ["618 Old Northern Road Dural 2158", "626 Old Northern Road Dural 2158", "21 Derriwong Road Dural 2158", "27 Derriwong Road Dural 2158", "614 Old Northern Road Dural 2158"]  
**Exhibition:** 2026-03-12 to 2026-04-28  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

The proposal aims to change the planning rules for properties at 614, 618, 626 Old Northern Road, and 21, 27 Derriwong Road, Dural [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.8]. Currently zoned for rural transition (RU6), the plan is to rezone these lands to allow for low-density residential housing (R2) [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.2]. This would enable the creation of 110 residential dwellings [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.2][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73]. Beyond new homes, the proposal includes several community benefits. It would create a new 4,000 square meter local park in the centre of the area, accessible to the community [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.2][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73][doc: PP-2024-450 - Urban Design Report | p.41]. Access to Dural Public School would be improved through new pedestrian connectivity via the park and pathways to the school site's frontage [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.35][doc: PP-2024-450 - Urban Design Report | p.41]. A new ‘drop-off and pick-up’ zone adjacent to the school on the local collector road would alleviate road congestion on Old Northern Road and create a safer environment for students and pedestrians [doc: PP-2024-450 - Traffic and Transport Impact Assessment | p.6][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.83][doc: PP-2024-450 - Traffic and Transport Impact Assessment | p.31]. Pedestrian links would also be dedicated and embellished to provide permeability for residents between local roads, through the local park, and to Old Northern Road [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.2][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.35][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73][doc: PP-2024-450 - Urban Design Report | p.41]. Traffic flow between Derriwong Road and Old Northern Road would be improved by a local collector road with two roundabouts, supporting a future Round Corner Bypass [doc: PP-2024-450 - Traffic and Transport Impact Assessment | p.6][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.83]. A new local collector road would be delivered within a 32-meter-wide road reserve, providing connection between Old Northern Road and Derriwong Road [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.2][doc: PP-2024-450 - Traffic and Transport Impact Assessment | p.6][doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Indicative Site Specific DCP | p.9][doc: PP-2024-450 - Urban Design Report | p.40][doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Indicative Site Specific DCP | p.10][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.35][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.83]. A new intersection would be constructed where the new local collector road meets Old Northern Road, subject to Transport for NSW requirements [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.4][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.35][doc: PP-2024-450 - Urban Design Report | p.31][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.83][doc: PP-2024-450 - Traffic and Transport Impact Assessment | p.8]. Additionally, the proposal includes providing a monetary contribution of $636,000 to Council towards active open space and $97,561 towards community facilities [doc: PP-2024-450 - Draft Public Benefit Offer | p.2][doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.35]. A strip of land along Old Northern Road would retain its SP2 Infrastructure (Classified Road) zoning to allow for future road widening [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.73].

## What changes on the ground

The planning proposal changes controls for the properties at 614, 618, 626 Old Northern Road, and 21, 27 Derriwong Road, Dural [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.42][doc: PP-2024-450 - Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.2]. Currently, these properties are zoned RU6 Transition [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.87]. The proposal seeks to change most of this to R2 Low Density Residential [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.87]. A narrow strip of land along the Old Northern Road frontage will remain zoned SP2 Infrastructure (Classified Road) to allow for future road widening [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.87]. Minimum lot sizes, which are currently 20,000m² (2 hectares), will be reduced and varied between 600m², 1,000m², and 2,000m² [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.33]. Larger lots are planned for steeper parts of the site [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.33]. A new rule will also limit residential development on the site to a maximum of 110 dwellings [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.9]. The proposal also includes improvements to the local road network [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.33] and the delivery of open space, including a new local park [doc: PP-2024-450 - Planning Proposal | p.33][doc: PP-2024-450 - Traffic and Transport Impact Assessment | p.50].

## Things to know

Here are some things to know about the technical studies for the proposal:

Road traffic noise was measured at 70 dB(A) during the day and 64 dB(A) at night at 614 Old Northern Road [doc: PP-2024-450 - Acoustic Assessment | p.7]. At 626 Old Northern Road, levels were 73 dB(A) day and 66 dB(A) night [doc: PP-2024-450 - Acoustic Assessment | p.7]. To manage noise, specific glazing and floor finishes are recommended for buildings [doc: PP-2024-450 - Acoustic Assessment | p.7][doc: PP-2024-450 - Acoustic Assessment | p.8]. If windows are kept closed, ventilation systems must meet building standards [doc: PP-2024-450 - Acoustic Assessment | p.7].

Contamination findings include elevated hydrocarbons in surface soils at 618 Old Northern Road [doc: PP-2024-450 - Detailed Site Investigation | p.39]. Asbestos fragments were found on a stockpile and in gravel fill near the dam at 618 Old Northern Road, with asbestos pipes identified extending about 120m east from the dam's northern bank [doc: PP-2024-450 - Detailed Site Investigation | p.39]. These impacts present a potential risk and require management [doc: PP-2024-450 - Detailed Site Investigation | p.39]. Water in the dam has elevated heavy metals, suitable for irrigation but not discharge into downstream waters [doc: PP-2024-450 - Detailed Site Investigation | p.39].

The proposal is not expected to harm the significance of any nearby heritage items [doc: PP-2024-450 - Heritage Impact Assessment | p.21]. No Aboriginal objects or archaeological sites were found in the study area [doc: PP-2024-450 - Aboriginal Heritage Assessment | p.22]. The land is not within a flood planning area [doc: PP-2024-450 - Detailed Site Investigation | p.167]. Any development in bushfire risk areas must comply with NSW Rural Fire Service guidelines [doc: PP-2024-450 - Detailed Site Investigation | p.167].
