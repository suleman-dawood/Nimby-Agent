# 94-98 Cosgrove Road, Strathfield South

**PP Number:** PP-2024-2431

## What's being proposed

This proposal is about changing the planning rules for the land at 94-98 Cosgrove Road, Strathfield South. The aim is to allow for the future development of a large, multi-level logistics facility [doc: Fact Sheet | p.2][doc: Planning Proposal (April 2026) | p.9].

Currently, the site is used for industrial and logistics purposes [doc: Fact Sheet | p.1]. The proposed changes would make it possible to build a three-storey warehousing facility [doc: PP Appendix N - Community Notification Letter | p.2]. This facility is planned to include two buildings, an open paved area in the middle for loading docks, and shared entry points for heavy vehicles [doc: PP Appendix N - Community Notification Letter | p.2]. It would also feature two circular ramps, allowing vehicles to access the first and second levels [doc: PP Appendix N - Community Notification Letter | p.2].

It's important to note that this proposal does not approve the construction of a specific building right now [doc: Frequently Asked Questions | p.3]. Instead, it amends the local planning rules to enable this type of development in the future [doc: Planning Proposal (April 2026) | p.9][doc: Planning Proposal (April 2026) | p.72]. Any actual building would require a separate development application at a later stage [doc: Frequently Asked Questions | p.2].

## What changes on the ground

The planning proposal for 94-98 Cosgrove Road, Strathfield South [doc: Fact Sheet | p.1][doc: PP Appendix G - Transport Assessment | p.18], will change some key development rules for the site. Currently, the land is zoned E4 General Industrial [doc: Fact Sheet | p.1] and this zoning will not change [doc: Frequently Asked Questions | p.3].

The main changes involve increasing the maximum allowed building height. Currently, buildings on the site are limited to 12 meters [doc: Planning Proposal (April 2026) | p.24][doc: Fact Sheet | p.2]. The proposal seeks to increase this maximum height to 35 meters [doc: Fact Sheet | p.2][doc: PP Appendix G - Transport Assessment | p.39].

The amount of floor space allowed on the site, known as Floor Space Ratio (FSR), will also increase. The current maximum FSR is 1:1 [doc: Planning Proposal (April 2026) | p.24][doc: Fact Sheet | p.2]. The proposal seeks to raise this to 1.6:1 [doc: Fact Sheet | p.2][doc: PP Appendix G - Transport Assessment | p.39]. This would allow for more building area on the 43,100m2 site [doc: Fact Sheet | p.1][doc: PP Appendix G - Transport Assessment | p.18].

Additionally, a new draft Site-Specific Development Control Plan (DCP) has been prepared [doc: PP Appendix G - Transport Assessment | p.14]. This DCP includes specific minimum building setbacks: 10 meters along Cosgrove Road, 7 meters along Hope Street, and 5 meters to Madeline Street [doc: Planning Proposal (April 2026) | p.55].

## Things to know

Residents should be aware of several potential impacts related to the planning proposal at 94-98 Cosgrove Road, Strathfield South. Traffic is expected to increase by up to 6% on the southern leg of Liverpool Road / Cosgrove Road [doc: PP Appendix H - Noise Impact Assessment | p.33] and up to 5% on the northern leg of Punchbowl Road / Cosgrove Road during peak hours [doc: PP Appendix H - Noise Impact Assessment | p.33]. However, traffic growth is not predicted to increase environmental noise by 2dB [doc: PP Appendix H - Noise Impact Assessment | p.33], and a traffic obstruction means no heavy vehicle movements are anticipated on the residential section of Madeline Street [doc: PP Appendix H - Noise Impact Assessment | p.33].

Operational noise from the proposed development is considered unlikely to cause sleep disturbance at nearby homes [doc: PP Appendix H - Noise Impact Assessment | p.32]. Nevertheless, construction activities have the potential to cause disruptive noise and dust, which can affect local amenity and health [doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.27][doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.24][doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.33]. Simple measures like screening, noise mitigation, and varied work hours are suggested during construction [doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.25].

The proposal also carries potential impacts on both Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal heritage items, including risks of 'cultural or spiritual loss' for Aboriginal heritage [doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.27]. Any future application will require specific investigations and reporting to mitigate these impacts [doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.27]. Further detailed traffic and noise assessments will also be required as part of any future Development Application for the site [doc: PP Appendix M - Social and Economic Impact Assessment | p.30].
