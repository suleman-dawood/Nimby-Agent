## What's being proposed

The GPT Group is proposing to redevelop the site at 580 George Street, Sydney [doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8]. The plan involves demolishing the existing three-level corner podium [doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8] and replacing it with a new 10-storey corner tower [doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8].

The existing 580 George Street tower will remain [doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8], as will its foyer, awning, servicing, parking arrangements, and the underground pedestrian connection to Town Hall Station [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.14].

The new 10-storey building will add approximately 8,000m2 of floor space [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.14]. This space will be used for vibrant retail shops on the lower levels and premium commercial offices on the upper levels [doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8]. The aim is to revitalise this key corner site, integrate with the area's heritage, and improve the urban design and experience for pedestrians [doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.12].

## What changes on the ground

The planning proposal for 580 George Street, Sydney, proposes specific changes to how buildings can be developed on the site, which is Lot 20 DP 835715 [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.32].

*   **Zoning:** There are no proposed changes to the site's current SP5 Metropolitan Centre zoning [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.3][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.14].
*   **Height Limits:** The existing maximum building height controls will remain unchanged, set at 55 metres for the north-west corner and 235 metres for the remainder of the site [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.3][doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.21].
*   **Floor Space Ratio (FSR):** The key change proposed is to the Floor Space Ratio (FSR). The site has a current mapped FSR of 8:1 [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.10][doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.21]. Through new site-specific provisions, the proposal seeks to increase the maximum FSR for the site to 15.1:1 [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.13][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.22][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.32]. This increase will allow for approximately 8,000m2 of additional employment floor space [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.14][doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8].
*   **Conditions for Increased FSR:** To be eligible for the 15.1:1 FSR, new development must not be used for residential accommodation or serviced apartments [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.13][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.32]. It must include end-of-journey facilities [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.13][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.32], demonstrate design excellence through an architectural design competition [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.13][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.22], and not result in additional car parking spaces on the land [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.13][doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.32].
*   **Lot Sizes:** The site area of 3,567m2 remains unchanged [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.3].
*   **New Infrastructure:** The proposal facilitates a new 10-storey building for office and retail uses [doc: Planning Proposal - 580 George Street Sydney | p.14][doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal Report_580 George Street, Sydney (1) | p.8].

## Things to know

The site at 580 George Street, Sydney, is not a listed heritage item but is located next to the State heritage-listed former Sydney Water Head Office (1939 Building) [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.45][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.7][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.38]. It is also near other state and local heritage items like the Town Hall, St Andrews Cathedral, and the Queen Victoria Building (QVB) [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.45][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.7].

A Heritage Impact Statement found that the proposed development would have no significant impact on the heritage items nearby [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.45][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.7]. This is because the design considers the local heritage context, proposing a lower-scale 3-storey podium to break up the building's size and mass [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.38][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.42]. A "waistline" at Level 03 helps divide the building horizontally [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.42]. The proposed changes affect only the podium, with no work on the existing tower [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.38][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.45].

To further reduce potential impacts, any future development will be guided by specific design rules in a Development Control Plan (DCP), a design competition, and ongoing heritage advice [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.41][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.43]. The DCP will include design principles for the building's form, height, and materials to ensure it respects the scale of nearby heritage items [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.41]. New materials should appear contemporary and not imitate historic building fabric [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.41].
