# Goulburn CBD Special Entertainment Precinct

**PP Number:** PP-2025-2551  
**Council:** Goulburn Mulwaree  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-03-30 to 2026-05-29  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

This proposal aims to create a Special Entertainment Precinct (SEP) in the Goulburn CBD [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9]. The main goal is to encourage more live performances and various entertainment venues within this area [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9].

The proposal intends to make the Goulburn CBD more lively both during the day and in the evening [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9]. It will also set clear sound limits for entertainment venues and fixed internal sound criteria for new apartments or mixed-use buildings to ensure reasonable comfort for residents [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9].

To achieve this, the plan proposes allowing businesses within the SEP to trade 24 hours a day, 7 days a week [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9]. It will also establish a clear process for handling complaints and define the roles of the Council, businesses, residents, Liquor and Gaming NSW, and NSW Police [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9]. This will be done by adding a new clause to the Goulburn Mulwaree Local Environmental Plan 2009, which will refer to a new map for the Goulburn CBD Special Entertainment Precinct [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.9]. This proposal is focused on trading hours and sound, and is not changing what types of land uses are allowed [doc: Attachment 3- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.19].

## What changes on the ground

This planning proposal creates a Special Entertainment Precinct (SEP) for the Goulburn CBD [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.5]. This means specific rules will apply to manage sound from entertainment venues and for new homes built in the area.

Specifically:
*   **Sound Controls:** New and existing entertainment venues must meet maximum external sound limits, defined by "Sound Category Areas" (SCA) [doc: Attachment 4- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.4]. These limits are measured outside the property boundary at 1.5 metres above the finished floor level for each floor, up to the maximum building height allowed (which could enable a 6-storey building) [doc: Attachment 4- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.4][doc: Goulburn-CBD-SEP_Development-Control-Plan-Policies_Nov-2025 (1) | p.4]. If a property has boundaries in different sound categories, the lower-numbered category applies to any undefined boundaries [doc: Attachment 4- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.4]. New residential properties will have internal sound limits applied through the Development Control Plan [doc: CBD SEP Planning Proposal_ Gateway Version_Jan 2026 | p.14].
*   **Other Controls:** This particular planning proposal does not introduce new land uses, zoning changes, or additional development standards for things like building height, floor space ratio (FSR), or minimum lot sizes beyond what is currently in the Goulburn Mulwaree Local Environmental Plan 2009 [doc: Attachment 3- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.22].

## Things to know

Here are the key things to know about the proposed changes near you:

The residential areas surrounding the Goulburn CBD Special Entertainment Precinct are mainly low-density, single-storey homes located within a Heritage Conservation Area [doc: Precinct-Management-Plan_Nov-2025_Goulburn-CBD-SEP (1) | p.9][doc: Attachment 5- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.9].

Rules for entertainment sound are assessed separately from noise caused by road and rail traffic [doc: Goulburn-CBD-SEP_Development-Control-Plan-Policies_Nov-2025 (1) | p.11][doc: Goulburn-CBD-SEP_Development-Control-Plan-Policies_Nov-2025 (1) | p.7]. Maximum entertainment sound levels are set for different Sound Category Areas and times of day [doc: CBD-SEP_Sound-Management-Framework | p.8][doc: Attachment 2- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.8]. For example, in the loudest area (SCA 1), entertainment sound must not exceed 70 dBA during the day/evening (7 am – 8 pm), 65 dBA early night (8 pm – 1 am), and 55 dBA late night (1 am – 7 am) [doc: CBD-SEP_Sound-Management-Framework | p.8][doc: Attachment 2- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.8]. Individual venues must operate slightly below these limits [doc: CBD-SEP_Sound-Management-Framework | p.8][doc: Attachment 2- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.8].

Existing background noise at locations like 73 Verner Street currently ranges from 27 dBA to 44 dBA, often due to local and distant traffic [doc: CBD-SEP_Sound-Management-Framework | p.56][doc: Attachment 2- Council Report- 16 December 2025 | p.56].

For new or modified homes, acoustic studies are required to show that building designs, materials, and other measures will ensure indoor noise levels remain within set limits [doc: Goulburn-CBD-SEP_Development-Control-Plan-Policies_Nov-2025 (1) | p.7]. If these soundproofing measures require windows to stay closed, alternative ventilation must be provided [doc: Goulburn-CBD-SEP_Development-Control-Plan-Policies_Nov-2025 (1) | p.7].
