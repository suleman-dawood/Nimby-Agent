# New Liverpool Local Environmental Plan

**PP Number:** PP-2024-1920  
**Council:** Liverpool  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-20 to 2026-06-12  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

The Liverpool City Council is proposing to create a new planning document, the New Liverpool Local Environmental Plan, to replace the existing Liverpool Local Environmental Plan 2008 [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.11]. This proposal applies to most of the land within the Liverpool Local Government Area, covering 29 residential suburbs, 11 industrial precincts, and numerous commercial centres across approximately 220 square kilometres [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.10]. The main goal is to update local planning rules to align with the strategic vision for the Western City District and Liverpool [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.11]. This includes rezoning four Council sites identified as pocket parks in the Liverpool City Centre Public Domain Masterplan to RE1 Public Recreation [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.34]. Additionally, 363 Council-owned parcels will be accurately re-zoned as RE1 Public Recreation [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.34]. For industrial areas, the proposal aims to define the individual role of 11 industrial precincts and establish flexible development standards to encourage renewal [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.12]. It also seeks to improve environmental protections, with a focus on water recycling, stormwater management, water sensitive urban design, urban heat, and biodiversity [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.12]. This includes introducing a new clause for stormwater and water sensitive urban design to reduce urban run-off and pollution [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.37]. Finally, it intends to correct existing errors and improve the clarity of planning clauses [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.12].

## What changes on the ground

This proposal includes specific changes to local planning controls. For **industrial areas**, several minimum lot size changes are proposed: * For land identified as part of Prestons/Yarrunga, the proposed minimum lot size for Heavy Industrial (IN3) will be 4,000 square metres [doc: Attachment 2 - Industrial Lands Investigation (Mecone, 2023) | p.11]. * For many industrial precincts, the proposal includes investigating changes to Floor Space Ratio (FSR) and height controls [doc: Attachment 2 - Industrial Lands Investigation (Mecone, 2023) | p.10]. For **Conservation zones (C2)**, a consistent minimum lot size of 40 hectares is proposed, differing from current varied requirements [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.15]. Also, "Recreation Area" will become a permitted use in C2 zones (with consent) [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.15]. New planning maps will be prepared for Land Zoning, Lot Size, FSR, and Height of Buildings [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.45][doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.14].

## Things to know

**Things to know** The proposal identifies several areas with environmental and hazard constraints. Many sites are identified as Bushfire Prone Land. This includes parts of Lot 4 Casula Road [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.46], Moorebank Avenue [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.45], Cabramatta Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.29], Cabrogal Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.41], Osmond Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.33], Lieutenant Cantello Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.34], and Peter Pan Park [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.35], all often in Category 1. Freeman Oval [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.31] and Durrant Oval [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.32] have some Bushfire Prone Land, including Category 1 and 3 areas. Havard Park & Pasquale Minnici Park also has some Bushfire Prone Land, including Category 2 and 3 [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.39]. Harris Creek Field has minimal Bushfire Prone Land, identified as a buffer [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.37]. Numerous locations across these sites face flood risk. The planning proposal applies to extensive land mapped as flood prone [doc: Planning Proposal Report | p.37]. Some areas face Low to High Flood Risk, including Lot 4 Casula Road [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.46], Moorebank Avenue [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.45], Cabrogal Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.41], Lieutenant Cantello Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.34], Havard Park & Pasquale Minnici Park [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.39], and Peter Pan Park [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.35]. Lot 100 Bapaume Road has a Low Flood Risk [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.44]. Several locations, such as Cabramatta Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.29], Osmond Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.33], Freeman Oval [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.31], and Durrant Oval [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.32], primarily experience High Flood Risk. Important ecological features such as Threatened Ecological Communities, wildlife corridors, riparian corridors, and key fish habitats are present across multiple sites. These features are found at Lot 4 Casula Road [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.46], Moorebank Avenue [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.45], Cabramatta Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.29], Cabrogal Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.41], Lieutenant Cantello Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.34], Havard Park & Pasquale Minnici Park [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.39], Peter Pan Park [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.35], Durrant Oval [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.32], Harris Creek Field [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.37], and Lot 100 Bapaume Road [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.44]. Osmond Reserve [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.33] and Freeman Oval [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.31] contain Threatened Ecological Communities, wildlife corridors, and riparian corridors. A biobanking agreement protects a 105-hectare biobank site for biodiversity offsets [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.45][doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.46]. These offset areas require active vegetation management [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.45]. Heritage item 11 (part of Lot 4 Casula Road) [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.46] and heritage item 57 (Moorebank Avenue) [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.45] are identified as constraints. A creek (zoned SP2 drainage) at Cabrogal Reserve may impact future use [doc: Attachment 14 - Endorsed Conservation Zones Study | p.41].
