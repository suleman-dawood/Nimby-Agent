# PP-2026-343 – Amendment to Signage controls within Coffs Harbour Local Environmental Plan 2013

**PP Number:** PP-2026-343  
**Council:** Coffs Harbour  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-09 to 2026-05-08  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

This planning proposal, PP-2026-343, is a plan to change the Coffs Harbour Local Environmental Plan (LEP) 2013 [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.4]. The main goal is to update the rules for signs throughout the entire Coffs Harbour local government area [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.6].

Currently, there's a conflict between the LEP and the Coffs Harbour Development Control Plan (DCP) 2015 regarding advertising signs that promote products or services not related to the business on the property (known as 'third-party advertising') [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.4][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.6]. The DCP intends to restrict these signs, but its provisions are not effective if they clash with the LEP [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.4][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.6].

To fix this, the proposal aims to prohibit "Signage (Advertising structures)" in several zones, including residential, commercial, industrial, mixed-use, tourist, and waterways areas [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.4][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.6]. However, it would still allow "Business identification signs" (signs that identify a business on its own premises) in many of these zones, provided Council gives its consent [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.4][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.6].

The overall intent is to improve the visual appeal, environmental quality, and urban design of the Coffs Harbour area by preventing distracting third-party advertising, while ensuring businesses can still clearly identify themselves [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.6].

## What changes on the ground

This proposal changes the rules for signage across several areas in the Coffs Harbour Local Government Area [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.5]. The main change is that 'third party advertising signage' (or 'advertising structures'), which were previously allowed with council approval in some zones, would now be prohibited [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.28][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.59][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.60]. This means signs that create visual clutter or light spill would no longer be permitted [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.59].

However, other types of signs will continue under existing rules. 'Business identification signs' would continue to be permitted [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.28]. Additionally, 'Building identification signs' would also continue to be permitted [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.28].

The provided documents do not indicate any changes to zoning boundaries [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.57][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.59][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.60][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.64] or the introduction of new infrastructure related to this proposal [doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.17][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.37][doc: Planning Proposal V2 - Exhibition COMPLETE | p.44].
