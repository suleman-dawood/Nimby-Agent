# Employment Lands Study - Short Term Recommendations 2025

**PP Number:** PP-2025-1608  
**Council:** Hornsby  
**Address:** []
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-13 to 2026-05-17  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

This planning proposal aims to update Hornsby Council's planning rules to boost economic activity and support future development of job-generating land across Hornsby Shire [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.6]. The main changes involve enabling different types of businesses and improving industrial areas. Specifically, the proposal will allow "light industries" in E1 Local Centre zones, but not in those areas identified as "Area 1" on the Land Zoning Map [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.7]. This means light industries will be permitted in larger local centres that were previously zoned B2 Local Centre [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.9]. This is intended to increase the diversity of jobs and opportunities in accessible locations [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.9]. For local centres, new rules will require active street frontages in the E1 Local Centre Zone [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.7][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.7]. The goal is to ensure businesses at street level attract pedestrian traffic and support the vitality of local centres [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.8][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.8], helping to create vibrant and safe places [doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.17]. The proposal also seeks to make industrial land more appealing for investment by allowing greater flexibility in new industrial designs and protecting these areas from other types of development [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.6]. There are no changes proposed for how flood-prone land is managed; existing rules will continue to apply [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.58].

## What changes on the ground

This proposal introduces several changes to planning rules that could affect local development.

For land zoned E1 Local Centre, new developments will need to include active shop fronts facing the street [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7]. Also, some light industries will now be allowed in the E1 Local Centre zone, but not in areas identified as “Area 1” on the Land Zoning Map [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7]. Serviced apartments will no longer count towards the ‘non-residential’ floorspace calculations [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7].

In areas zoned E4 General Industrial, there are a few changes. The maximum size for new food and drink businesses will be limited to 150 square metres of gross floor area [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7]. Building height limits could increase up to a maximum of 20 metres in some of these industrial areas [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7][doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.12]. Current limits in E4 General Industrial areas are 10.5m (Area K) or 14.5m (Area N) [doc: Draft amendments to the HDCP 2024 - July 2025 | p.4].

Specifically for the Dural Industrial Precinct, the maximum Floor Space Ratio (FSR) is proposed to increase from the current 0.7:1 to 1:1 [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7][doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.14]. This change aims to align Dural with other E4 General Industrial areas like Mount Kuring-Gai, Thornleigh, and Asquith, which already have a 1:1 FSR [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.14].
