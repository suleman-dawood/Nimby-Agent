## What's being proposed

This planning proposal aims to update the Hornsby Local Environmental Plan (LEP) to boost economic activity and improve how employment land is used across Hornsby Shire [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.6]. This is intended to increase job opportunities and make these areas more accessible and appealing [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.6]. The proposal will also require active street frontages in local centres [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.7]. This is intended to attract pedestrian traffic [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.8][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.8][doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.7] and support the vitality and vibrancy of these areas [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.17][doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.8][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.8][doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.6]. Additionally, the changes seek to improve the use and appeal of industrial land, protect it from other types of development, and allow more flexible designs for new industrial buildings to meet modern business needs [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.6][doc: Planning Proposal ELS | p.6].

## What changes on the ground

If this proposal is adopted, you would see specific changes to planning controls in certain areas.

In the E1 Local Centre zone, light industries would become permissible, excluding areas identified as “Area 1” on the Land Zoning Map [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7]. New buildings in this zone would also be required to have an "active street frontage" [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7].

For some E4 General Industrial zoned precincts, the maximum building height control could increase to 20 metres [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7]. Also, within the E4 General Industrial zone, food and drink premises would be limited to 150 square metres of gross floor area [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7][doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.11].

Specifically for the Dural Industrial Precinct, the maximum Floor Space Ratio (FSR) would increase from 0.7:1 to 1:1 [doc: Planning Proposal ELS - RFS Consultation | p.7][doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.14]. For residential accommodation, new FSR controls would apply to specific areas: Area 3 at 1:1 and Area 6 at 0.6:1 [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.11]. For shop top housing, FSRs in 'Area 5' and 'Area 8' could exceed mapped limits if combined with other specified non-residential uses [doc: Employment Lands Study Planning Proposal - July 2025 | p.11].
