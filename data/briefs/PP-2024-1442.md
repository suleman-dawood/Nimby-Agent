# Planning Proposal to introduce an Additional Permitted Use to 1 Highworth Avenue, Bexley

**PP Number:** PP-2024-1442  
**Council:** Bayside  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-02 to 2026-05-05  
**Type:** council-led

## What's being proposed

This planning proposal, PP-2024-1442, suggests changing the planning rules for the property at 1 Highworth Avenue, Bexley [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1]. The site is currently a 542m² block with a single detached house, zoned R2 Low Density Residential [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1].

The proposal aims to introduce an "Additional Permitted Use" to the Bayside Local Environmental Plan (LEP) 2021 [doc: Planning Proposal | p.12][doc: Planning Proposal | p.13]. This change would allow the property to be redeveloped to provide additional car parking [doc: Planning Proposal | p.12]. This new parking area would support the operational needs of the McDonald's fast food restaurant, which is located next door to the south [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1][doc: Planning Proposal | p.12]. The plan is to add 12 new car parking spaces, bringing the total for the combined site to 41 spaces [doc: Planning Proposal | p.12]. This is intended to help reduce vehicle queues on the street during busy periods and address traffic impact and congestion [doc: Planning Proposal | p.12][doc: Planning Proposal | p.13].

## What changes on the ground

This proposal introduces specific changes for the property at 1 Highworth Avenue, Bexley, legally identified as Lot 26 DP8760 [doc: Planning Proposal | p.10][doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1]. Currently, the site is 575m² [doc: Planning Proposal | p.10] (or 542m² [doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1][doc: Planning Proposal | p.12]) and contains a single-storey dwelling, zoned R2 Low Density Residential [doc: Planning Proposal | p.10][doc: Exhibition Fact Sheet | p.1]. The main change is that "take away food and drink premises" would become a permitted land use on this specific site, but only with Council approval [doc: Planning Proposal | p.13]. On the ground, this change aims to allow for additional car parking space to support the neighbouring commercial operation [doc: Planning Proposal | p.13]. It also allows for future redevelopment of the site to address traffic and congestion [doc: Planning Proposal | p.13]. The single-storey dwelling on the site is proposed for demolition [doc: Noise Impact Assessment | p.7]. This redevelopment could include improving the site's access point by moving it further from the Forest Road intersection to enhance safety [doc: Traffic Assessment | p.1][doc: Frequently Asked Questions | p.3]. The planning proposal states that this change would not prevent the site from maintaining its potential for residential purposes [doc: Planning Proposal | p.27]. There are no proposed changes to building height limits, floor space ratios, or minimum lot sizes for the property in these documents [doc: Planning Proposal | p.27].

## Things to know

Here's what you should know about the technical studies:

A Heritage Impact Statement found that the proposal involves demolishing the house at 1 Highworth Avenue and extending a carpark at 543 Forest Road [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.19][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.5]. While these properties are not heritage listed, they are near two local heritage items: "Street plantings" and "Bexley Fire Station" at 163 Queen Victoria Street [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.5][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.7]. The demolition and carpark extension would be visible from these heritage items [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.19]. However, the study concludes the works will not impact their streetscapes, views, or significance [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.19][doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.20]. The considerable distance between the works and heritage items ensures minimal effect [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.23]. This heritage assessment did not include Aboriginal and Archaeological impact assessment [doc: Heritage Impact Statement | p.7].

A Noise Impact Assessment conducted between April 30 and May 10, 2024 [doc: Noise Impact Assessment | p.19], determined that existing road traffic, commercial activities, and aircraft noise already dominate the surrounding environment [doc: Noise Impact Assessment | p.19][doc: Noise Impact Assessment | p.20]. The assessment considered influences of traffic noise where existing levels are high [doc: Noise Impact Assessment | p.13].
