## What's being proposed

The planning proposal aims to change the classification of the subject land from ‘community’ land to ‘operational’ land [doc: Attachment A - Planning Proposal | p.8]. This change is intended to align the land's classification with its current use as a car park [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.9].

Reclassifying the land would provide flexibility for the current car park use and allow Council to consider opportunities for future development proposals, including potential housing [doc: Council report 24 March 2025 Attachment 1 | p.8][doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.15]. This would also remove a number of limitations or restrictions currently applying to the land [doc: Attachment A - Planning Proposal | p.8]. The proposal facilitates opportunities for other uses on the site, capitalising on its current underutilisation as a car park [doc: Council report 24 March 2025 Attachment 1 | p.12].

Importantly, the proposal does not change the current zoning or planning controls for the site [doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.12]. Reclassification does not commit Council to selling or developing the land, nor does it remove Council’s ownership or prevent current uses from continuing [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.9]. There is no associated plan to remove the car park [doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.12]. Any future changes would require separate decisions and processes [doc: Attachment A - Planning Proposal | p.8].

## What changes on the ground

This planning proposal changes the classification of the land at 9A Eastern Valley Way, Northbridge, from "Community" to "Operational" [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.4]. This reclassification allows Council to consider more types of commercial partnerships and arrangements for future redevelopment of the site [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.4].

It's important to understand that this proposal does *not* set any new specific controls for zoning, building height limits, or Floor Space Ratio (FSR) at this time [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.4][doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.4]. Council previously decided to keep the existing controls for the Northbridge Plaza and Car Park, and the wider shopping centre [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.4][doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.4].

Instead, this reclassification is an initial step [doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.4]. Any potential future changes to planning controls for the site, including zoning, height, or FSR, would happen later through a separate "master planning process" [doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.4]. This future process would involve more community and stakeholder engagement and technical investigations [doc: Planning proposal Willoughby reclassification for exhibition | p.4][doc: Amended Planning Proposal - Re-exhibition April 2026 | p.4].
