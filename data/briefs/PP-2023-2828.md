# Kurnell Peninsula

**PP Number:** PP-2023-2828  
**Council:** Sutherland Shire  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-10 to 2026-05-11  
**Type:** state-led

## What's being proposed

This proposal, known as the Kurnell Peninsula Rezoning Proposal, aims to rezone land at 251, 260R, 278, and 280–282 Captain Cook Drive, Kurnell [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.7][doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5][doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.6]. The vision is to enable the staged development of a new, mixed-use community for the Sutherland Shire [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5][doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.18].

The project proposes approximately 4,300 new homes, including affordable and seniors housing, along with tourist accommodation and retail [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5]. It also includes 140 hectares of green space, dedicating two kilometres of beach along Boat Harbour and Bate Bay as public open space [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5]. Additionally, three district parks and nine local parks will be dedicated to the Council as public open space, along with public roads and beachfront parking areas [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.24]. These changes require amending existing planning controls within the Sutherland Shire Local Environmental Plan 2015 and State Environmental Planning Policy (Precincts—Central River City) 2021 [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.14][doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.7].

## What changes on the ground

The proposal introduces new zoning across the site [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. This includes RE2 Private Recreation for open space [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25], SP2 Infrastructure for water and sewer facilities at Lot 2 South's western edge [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25], SP3 Tourist in two areas for hotels and cabins [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25], R3 Medium Density Residential for duplexes and terraces [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25], R4 High Density Residential for 2 to 12-storey residential flat buildings [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25], and E1 Local Centre for a new town centre with approximately 7,000m2 of retail space, including a supermarket [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. Six 12-storey buildings are limited to the Town Centre and Bate Bay precincts [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.34]. The current minimum lot size of one hectare for Lots 8 and 9 [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.17] is proposed to be removed for the site [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.17]. However, specific guidance applies, such as a minimum resultant lot size of 185sqm for Multi Dwelling Housing [doc: Draft Design Guidelines | p.27] and a minimum lot width of 25m for medium density buildings [doc: Draft Design Guidelines | p.29][doc: Draft Design Guidelines | p.30].

## Things to know

Residents should know about several potential impacts.

**Traffic and Noise:** Construction activities may lead to local noise, dust, odours, and increased vehicle movements [doc: Social Impact Assessment - Dec_23 | p.44]. Increased vehicle noise is an identified social impact [doc: Social Impact Assessment - Dec_23 | p.43]. Traffic assessments include early evacuation plans for Captain Cook Drive, which provides two evacuation directions [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.78]. Road upgrades are proposed to aid emergency responses [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.78].

**Bushfire:** A Strategic Bushfire Study found "low risk development outcomes," but the Department raised concerns about how risk was quantified [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.81]. Some residual bushfire risk will always remain [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.21]. Evacuation is a key part of the bushfire response, particularly for vulnerable uses like proposed hotels and seniors living [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.78][doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.81].

**Flooding:** The proposal must align with the new Flood Risk Management Manual 2023 [doc: Geotechnical Factors Report-Part-2 - Nov_23 | p.12]. A Flood Impact and Risk Assessment has been completed [doc: Water, Ecology, Landscape Integration Strategy - Sept_25 | p.25].

**Contamination:** An investigation report is needed to confirm the site is suitable for its planned uses [doc: Geotechnical Factors Report-Part-2 - Nov_23 | p.12]. Preliminary soil sampling is required for areas not yet rehabilitated with Virgin Excavated Natural Material [doc: Stormwater Management Report - Sept_25 | p.45].

**Heritage and Ecology:** Studies have assessed potential impacts on Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal heritage items [doc: Social Impact Assessment - Dec_23 | p.44], and ecological features such as the Green and Golden Bell Frog [doc: Water, Ecology, Landscape Integration Strategy - Sept_25 | p.25].
