# Kurnell Peninsula

**PP Number:** PP-2023-2828  
**Council:** Sutherland Shire  
**Address:** []  
**Exhibition:** 2026-04-10 to 2026-05-11  
**Type:** state-led

## What's being proposed

This proposal, known as the Kurnell Peninsula Rezoning Proposal, seeks to change the planning rules for land at 251, 260R, 278, and 280-282 Captain Cook Drive, Kurnell [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.7][doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5]. The overall vision is to enable the staged development of a new, mixed-use community [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5].

If approved, the changes would allow for approximately 4,300 new homes, including affordable and seniors housing, along with tourist accommodation and retail [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5]. The masterplan includes 140 hectares of green space, featuring the dedication of two kilometres of beach along Boat Harbour and Bate Bay as public open space [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.5]. Additionally, three district parks and nine local parks would be dedicated to Sutherland Shire Council for public use, alongside public roads and beachfront parking areas [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.24]. The proposal aims to protect and enhance the cultural and environmental qualities of the site, regenerating the natural environment and celebrating First Nations cultural history through practices, storytelling, and cultural activities [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.7][doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.18].

## What changes on the ground

The proposal will introduce new zoning across the site. These lots, plus Lot 2 South, will see new zones like RE2 Private Recreation for ecological corridors, active tracks, and stormwater systems [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. SP2 Infrastructure will be created for water and sewer infrastructure [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. Two areas will be zoned SP3 Tourist for hotels and cabins [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. Residential zones will include R3 Medium Density Residential for duplexes and terraces, and R4 High Density Residential for apartment buildings [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. A new E1 Local Centre zone will introduce a town centre with approximately 7,000m² of retail and commercial space, including a supermarket [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.25]. The current minimum lot size of one hectare for Lots 8 and 9 will be removed [doc: Explanation of Intended Effects | p.17][doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.26]. For new Multi Dwelling Housing like terraces, a minimum lot size of 185sqm and a minimum width of 7m will apply [doc: Draft Design Guidelines | p.27]. Building height limits will change significantly. Six 12-storey buildings are limited to the Town Centre and Bate Bay precincts [doc: Rezoning Proposal | p.34].

## Things to know

Here are things to know about the planning proposal's potential impacts and conditions:

If the proposal goes ahead, residents could experience social impacts like changes to their way of life due to increased vehicle movement and noise [doc: Social Impact Assessment - Dec_23 | p.43]. During construction, local amenity might be affected by noise, dust, odours, and vehicle traffic [doc: Social Impact Assessment - Dec_23 | p.44].

For **bushfire**, a strategic study considered risks to proposed hotels, seniors living, and a new school, as well as existing risks from the oil refinery and desalination plant [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.81]. Emergency response will focus on evacuation, with Captain Cook Drive providing multiple routes and planned upgrades [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.78]. Be aware that some residual bushfire risk will always remain [doc: Strategic Bushfire Study - May_25 | p.21].

**Flooding** studies are required, and the new Flood Risk Management Manual 2023 must be addressed [doc: Geotechnical Factors Report-Part-2 - Nov_23 | p.12]. **Contamination** investigations and soil sampling are needed to ensure the site is suitable for future uses [doc: Geotechnical Factors Report-Part-2 - Nov_23 | p.12][doc: Stormwater Management Report - Sept_25 | p.45]. **Air quality** impacts were assessed, with further modelling potentially needed for high-risk areas before rezoning [doc: Stormwater Management Report - Sept_25 | p.45]. Studies also cover **Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal heritage** [doc: Social Impact Assessment - Dec_23 | p.44] and **ecological impacts**, including a Green and Golden Bell Frog Report [doc: Water, Ecology, Landscape Integration Strategy - Sept_25 | p.25].
